Privacy Policy for SortSatisfy

Last updated: May 16, 2026

SortSatisfy ("we", "us", "our") is a mobile game published by Sanket Bodhare. This Privacy Policy explains what information is collected when you use the SortSatisfy mobile application (the "App"), how that information is used, and your choices regarding it.

Information the App Collects Directly

The App does not collect personally identifiable information directly. All game progress, settings, and preferences are stored locally on your device in a save file. We do not maintain server-side accounts or profiles.

Third-Party Services

The App uses the following third-party services, each with its own privacy practices:

  • Google Play Services. Google's standard platform services for Android apps. See Google's privacy policy.
  • Google AdMob. We use AdMob to display rewarded video advertisements in the App. AdMob automatically collects the following data for advertising, analytics, and fraud prevention purposes:
    • Your device's IP address (used to estimate general location)
    • Information about your interactions with ads (app launches, taps, video views)
    • Diagnostic information about app performance (such as launch time and energy usage)
    • Your Android Advertising ID and app set ID
    All data collected by AdMob is encrypted in transit using TLS. See AdMob's data disclosures for full details.

Your Data Rights and How to Delete Your Data

You have the right to delete your data at any time. SortSatisfy stores most of your data locally on your device, so deletion is straightforward.

Local Game Data (Progress, Settings, Coins)

All your game progress, settings, earned coins, daily streak, and preferences are stored only on your device. To delete this data, choose either option:

  • Uninstall the App: open your device's app settings, find SortSatisfy, and tap Uninstall. All local game data is deleted immediately.
  • Clear App Data: open Android Settings, go to Apps, tap SortSatisfy, then Storage, then Clear Data. Your save file is deleted but the App remains installed.

Advertising Data (AdMob)

AdMob may store your Android Advertising ID and information about ads you have interacted with. To delete or reset this data:

  • Reset your Advertising ID: open Android Settings, go to Privacy or Google, tap Ads, then tap Reset advertising ID.
  • Opt out of personalized ads: in the same Ads settings screen, toggle Opt out of Ads Personalization.
  • Manage your Google Ad Settings: visit adssettings.google.com to view and delete data Google has collected for advertising purposes.

Other Requests or Questions

If you have any other data deletion requests, privacy questions, or concerns that are not addressed above, contact us by email:

Email: sortsatisfy@gmail.com

We will respond to data deletion requests within 30 days. Please include your device type and a brief description of your request so that we can help you efficiently.

Rights of Users in the European Union and United Kingdom

If you are located in the European Union (EU), European Economic Area (EEA), or United Kingdom (UK), you have specific rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and UK GDPR:

  • Right to access: request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification: correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to erasure: request deletion of your data (the "right to be forgotten")
  • Right to data portability: receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Right to object: object to processing for direct marketing or based on legitimate interests
  • Right to withdraw consent: opt out of personalized advertising at any time
  • Right to lodge a complaint: contact your local data protection authority

Most data SortSatisfy uses is stored locally on your device, so most rights can be exercised directly through the data deletion methods described above. For any other requests, contact us at sortsatisfy@gmail.com. We respond within 30 days.

Rights of California Residents

If you are a California resident, you have rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA):

  • Right to know what personal information is collected, used, shared, or sold
  • Right to delete personal information we hold about you
  • Right to correct inaccurate personal information
  • Right to opt-out of the sale or sharing of personal information. We do not sell personal information in the traditional sense, but the sharing of advertising identifiers with AdMob's advertising partners may be considered a "sale" or "sharing" under CCPA. You can opt out using the Advertising ID controls described above.
  • Right to non-discrimination for exercising your rights

To exercise these rights, contact us at sortsatisfy@gmail.com.

International Data Transfers

SortSatisfy uses Google services (AdMob and Google Play Services) which may transfer your data to servers located outside your country of residence, including the United States. Google maintains compliance with applicable data transfer mechanisms, such as Standard Contractual Clauses for EU data transfers. See Google's privacy policy for details on their data transfer practices.

Advertising

The App displays rewarded video advertisements served by Google AdMob. These ads are optional. They appear only when you actively tap a "Watch Ad" button within the App to earn in-game rewards. We do not display interstitial or banner ads that interrupt gameplay.

AdMob may use your device's Advertising ID to show personalized ads. You control this through your device settings, as described in the data deletion section above. If you opt out of personalized ads, you will still see ads in SortSatisfy when you tap "Watch Ad", but they will not be personalized to your interests.

Children's Privacy

The App is intended for users aged 16 and above. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe that a child under 13 has provided us with personal information, please contact us at sortsatisfy@gmail.com so that we can investigate and take appropriate action.
